About Mozambique

Mozambique, officially designated as the Republic of Mozambique, is a country located in the southeast of the African continent, bathed by the Indian Ocean to the east and bordering Tanzania to the north;
Malawi and Zambia to the northwest; Zimbabwe to the west and South Africa to the southwest.
The capital and largest city in the country is Maputo, formerly called Lourenço Marques during Portuguese rule.

General Description

The country is divided into 11 provinces in the north, center and south respectively.

Gastronomy

Badjias

Badjias are fried snacks, typical of Indian cuisine, but also very popular in Mozambique, where they are usually made with Nhemba Bean flour and sold on the street along with bread, to serve as a breakfast. The flour is mixed with yeast, salt, crushed garlic, and sometimes other seasonings;

Xiguinha

Xiguinha are dish originating in southern Mozambique, typical of the province of Inhambane. made with Cassava, Peanut, Cacana or Nhemba beans and Coconut milk and sometimes with clams;

Frango à Zambeziana

Frango à Zambeziana, its a reference of Mozambican gastronomy the secret of this recipe is to season the chicken with garlic, lemon and coconut milk, which after marinating, the chicken is grilled and when instead we put the milk preparation and olive oil to make the skin crispier

Pumpkin leaves

Pumpkin leaves (Mboa) Before washing the leaves, remove the fibers from the stalk and ribs, pulling with a knife. Roll up the leaves and chop. Place the washed and chopped leaves, tomato, pepper, onion, curry, salt and water in a pan. Mix, cover and cook over low heat for 15 minutes or until the vegetables are tender with some broth. Add the peanut butter and mix. Let it boil again and turn off the heat.

Matapa

Matapa is, in Mozambique, the dish made with pounded cassava leaves, cooked in a sauce based on pounded peanuts and coconut milk and seasoned with shellfish, which can be shrimp (fresh or dried) or crab. It is usually served with xima (cooked corn flour) or white rice

Dried fish

Dried fish Well smoked, salted and dried, the fish can be preserved for over a year. and before consuming it is immersed in water for a while to reduce the salt and it is cooked normally (fried or with tomato sauce, accompanying with Xima)

Natural resources

  • Niassa : Coal potential in the Lago e Cobre district;

  • C. Delgado : Ruby in Montepuez; large deposits of vanadium-associated graphite at Balame and Ancuambe; gas and oil depot, marbles in Palma and Mocimboa ;

  • Nampula : Occurrence of base metals (copper, nickel and zinc) associated with Gold Vanadium and silver in Monapo and Murrupula: heavy sands in Moma and Angoche, Phosphorus, mineral water, Iron and limestone ;

  • Zambézia : Occurrence of Gold, heavy sands, Bauxie ;

  • Tete : Coal, Titanium; graphite and phosphate;

  • Manica : Gold, Copper, Nickel, Mineral Water;

  • Inhambane : Natural gas (Pande and Temane) and heavy sands.
